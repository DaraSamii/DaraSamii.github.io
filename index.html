About Me

I'm a Master's student in Mechanical Engineering with a background in Chemical Engineering, focused on integrating machine learning with computational fluid dynamics.

My research explores how Graph Neural Networks can accelerate CFD simulations, achieving up to 100× speedup over traditional solvers. I'm particularly interested in thermal spray coating prediction using aeroacoustic signals.

Beyond research, I lead CFD simulations for SpaceConcordia's solid rocketry team and hold an L2 rocketry certification from the Tripoli Rocketry Association.

SpaceConcordia CR25 "Aether" CFD Analysis

CR25 "Aether" was SpaceConcordia's 2025 rocket with a unique transition design. Due to its unconventional shape, classic equations and load analysis weren't applicable, so an OpenFOAM case was developed to solve flow governing equations on the rocket and determine centre of pressure, rotational and translational acceleration, and bending forces on the rocket airframe.

OpenFOAM CFD ParaView
GitHub

Compressible Cold Spray Aeroacoustic Simulation

Developed compressible high-pressure, high-temperature solver with Lagrangian particle tracking (sonicDPMFoam) and DDES for simulating cold spray nozzle. Used libAcoustics and FW-H analogy to capture aeroacoustic noise generated by jet and track in-flight particles.

OpenFOAM DDES Aeroacoustics DPM
